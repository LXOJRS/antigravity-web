Shape ideas forward

I help people turn ideas into decisions that actually hold up in practice.

Most teams I work with don’t struggle with ambition or intelligence. They struggle with translation. Ideas sound good in theory, but fall apart once they meet real workflows, real people, and real constraints. AI has made that gap more visible, and harder to ignore.

That’s where I come in.

What I do

I work with professionals across sectors, from corporates and government to education and the creative industry, to help them think, decide, and create more deliberately with AI.

Not by handing out prompts or tools, but by teaching people how to work with machine output without outsourcing their judgment. AI doesn’t replace thinking. It challenges it. My role is to help teams stay in control of decisions that actually matter.

Where this comes from

My background is not technical by default. I studied film, rhetoric, and business strategy, where I learned how meaning is shaped: how language carries assumptions, how structure guides interpretation, and how stories quietly influence decisions.

During my cum laude Master’s in Media & Business at Erasmus University, I translated that analytical lens to organizational strategy and value creation. That perspective turned out to be essential for AI work.

Because AI doesn’t give answers.
It gives suggestions. Patterns. Language.
And someone still has to decide what those outputs mean, and what to do with them.

How I approach AI

I developed the Value Fit model as part of my cum laude Master’s thesis to help teams figure out where AI genuinely fits their goals and culture, and where it doesn’t.

I apply that thinking in training sessions, strategic frameworks, and real projects where clarity matters more than speed.

I’m interested in the practical, often messy work of figuring out what’s next, without pretending it’s simple.

If that sounds familiar, we’ll probably have a good conversation.
